Ensaymadas

This dish is a Filipino variation of the Spanish pastry known as ensaïmada. The Filipino version originated during the Spanish colonial era, but the traditional Spanish Ensaimada de Mallorca dates back to the 17th century. Over time, Filipino bakers adapted it using local ingredients, transforming it into the soft, buttery, cheesy pastry widely loved today.

The Recipe

The Filipino ensaymada is a soft, sweet brioche-like pastry brushed with butter, rolled in sugar, and topped with grated cheese. It's often eaten as a snack or paired with hot chocolate or coffee.

Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered milk

For the Toppings

  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 1 cup sugar (for coating)
  • Grated cheddar cheese

Directions

  1. Activate the yeast by mixing it with warm milk and warm water. Let it foam.
  2. Combine flour, sugar, salt, and powdered milk.
  3. Add egg yolks and the yeast mixture.
  4. Knead until smooth, then add softened butter.
  5. Let the dough rise until doubled.
  6. Roll into coils or spirals and place on a tray.
  7. Let rise again, then bake at 350°F until lightly golden.
  8. Brush with softened butter, roll in sugar, and top with grated cheese.
