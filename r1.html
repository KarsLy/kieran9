The Filipino ensaymada was introduced to the Philippines during the Spanish colonial period, but the original Spanish ensaimada de Mallorca dates back to at least the 17th century. While the Spanish version was brought to the islands, Filipino bakers later adapted it by adding local ingredients, transforming it into its own unique pastry.

The Recipe

The Filipino ensaymada is a beloved Filipino soft, sweet, brioche-like pastry that is covered with butter, sugar, and topped with grated cheese.

Ingredients

    For the dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered milk (optional but reccomended)

    • For toppings

  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 1 cup sugar (for coating)
  • Grated cheddar cheese
