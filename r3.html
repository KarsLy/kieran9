Ensaymadas

A Filipino Dessert

This dish is a Filipino variation of the Spanish pastry known as ensaïmada. The Filipino version originated during the Spanish colonial era, but the traditional Spanish Ensaimada de Mallorca dates back to the 17th century. Over time, Filipino bakers adapted it using local ingredients, transforming it into the soft, buttery, cheesy pastry widely loved today.

The Filipino ensaymada is a soft, sweet brioche-like pastry brushed with butter, rolled in sugar, and topped with grated cheese. It's often eaten as a snack or paired with hot chocolate or coffee.


The Recipe


Ingredients

For the Dough

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 1/4 tsp active dry yeast
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 4 egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup powdered milk

For the Toppings

  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 1 cup sugar (for coating)
  • Grated cheddar cheese


Step One

Activate the yeast by mixing it with warm milk and warm water. Let it foam.

Step Two

Combine flour, sugar, salt, and powdered milk.

Step Three

Add egg yolks and the yeast mixture.

Step Four

Knead until smooth, then add softened butter.

Step Five

Let the dough rise until doubled.

Step Six

Roll into coils or spirals and place on a tray.

Step Seven

Let rise again, then bake at 350°F until lightly golden.

Step Eight

Brush with softened butter, roll in sugar, and top with grated cheese.
